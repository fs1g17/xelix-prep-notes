### Speed vs Quality

- Speed and quality are not opposites; poor prioritisation is usually the real problem.
- Moving fast means making explicit trade-offs about scope, risk, and reversibility.
- At Ai-UP, we shipped desktop-first because it matched the highest-value MVP usage.
- We deferred responsiveness, but protected maintainability and future flexibility.
- The MVP reached customers in just over four weeks and later scaled without a frontend rewrite.
- At Capgemini, rapid prototypes helped validate ideas before the team committed to costly paths.
- I protect quality most on correctness, maintainability, tests, security, and user-critical workflows.
- My approach is to protect the foundations, be pragmatic on scope, and avoid hidden debt.

### Handling Ambiguity

- Ambiguity is normal, so I try to turn it into something concrete quickly.
- I start by clarifying the goal, constraints, users, and risks.
- If the answer is still unclear, I make a sensible assumption and validate it fast.
- Prototypes give design, product, and engineering something shared to react to.
- At Capgemini, prototyping exposed CASA framework constraints before the team committed.
- That helped us rule out an infeasible flow early and avoid rework later.
- I communicate assumptions clearly so people can challenge them.
- My approach is to move forward without pretending uncertain things are certain.

### Disagreement

- Disagreement should be in service of the outcome, not personal preference.
- The shared goal is customer value, not proving my idea is right.
- I speak up when I see a meaningful risk, opportunity, or clearer approach.
- I frame disagreements around users, trade-offs, evidence, and constraints.
- I am comfortable contributing ideas outside my strict lane, especially around UX.
- If the team chooses another direction, I ask why so I can understand the reasoning.
- Once a decision is made, I commit fully and help execute it properly.
- Being a strong teammate matters more than being right.

### Testing Philosophy

- Testing should help the team move faster with confidence.
- Good tests protect important behaviour, not implementation details.
- I would not chase 100% coverage blindly.
- I prioritise critical paths, business logic, edge cases, and regression-prone areas.
- At Ai-UP, we use RTL, Jest, and Nock for important frontend and API flows.
- Playwright-style e2e tests are valuable for high-risk user journeys.
- TDD works best when behaviour is already well understood.
- In ambiguous product work, I prefer to stabilise the feature shape before locking tests around it.
